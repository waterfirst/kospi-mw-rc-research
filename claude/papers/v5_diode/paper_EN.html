A Diode Model of KOSPI Market Dynamics:
Refuting the RC Monotonic-Recovery Hypothesis with a 2-Year Backtest

Claude (analysis, model, code) · Cheom-mul (design, review)
2026-06-26 · Research purpose only · Not investment advice · Data: Naver Finance (KOSPI daily price & investor-type net flows)
Abstract. We identify why the prior RC (resistor–capacitor) monotonic-recovery model fails structurally during the June 2026 KOSPI crash–recovery episode, and propose an RLC + diode model. The central hypothesis is a directional diode D_sell = (foreign net-sell AND institutional net-sell) that ignites market crashes. In a backtest over 604 trading days (2024–2026), days with D_sell ON had a mean daily return of −1.19% versus +0.68% when OFF, a 1.87 pp gap with an overwhelming t = 9.55. The diode captured 83% of crash days (≤ −5%), and its directional classification accuracy on large-move days (≥ 3%) was 82.4%. We honestly report that D_sell is contemporaneous with the close, so it has no next-day point-forecast power (mean reversion dominates). The RC model cannot simultaneously explain the rallies of Jun 24–25 (rising amid foreign selling) and the Jun 26 crash (joint selling); the diode model classifies both with statistical significance.

1. Introduction — Structural Failure of RC Monotonic Recovery

Prior RC models (v3, v7) described KOSPI recovery as a driven RC relaxation:

C_eff · dV/dt = σ_total · (V_target − V) − I_forced solution (no forced sell): V(t) = V_target − (V_target−V₀)·e^(−t/τ)

This form forces V to converge monotonically toward V_target. Yet the one-day reversal from 2026-06-25 (+5.42%, 8,930) to 06-26 (−5.81%, 8,411) is irreproducible by a monotonic solution. RC omits three physical quantities:

  • (i) Inertia (L): program/leverage selling carries momentum; RC has no second-order term.
  • (ii) Directional asymmetry (diode): joint foreign+institutional selling differs qualitatively from foreign-only selling.
  • (iii) Foreign-gate fallacy: RC v7 treats the foreign sign as master switch, but Jun 24–25 rallied during foreign selling.

2. The Diode Model

We add a directional diode in series with an RLC circuit:

L·d²V/dt² + R·dV/dt + (V − V_target)/C = −D_sell(t)·K·|F+I|/C_flow D_sell(t) = 1 iff F(t) < 0 AND I(t) < 0 = 0 otherwise

F, I are foreign/institutional daily net flows. The diode conducts only during joint (panic) selling, igniting the shock term. For damping ratio ζ < 1 the system is underdamped, naturally producing the overshoot/oscillation that RC's monotonic convergence cannot.

Empirical calibration: C_flow = 0.017 tril/pt, K_cascade = 1.78 (D+0 cascade fit), τ = 2.32 days.

3. Data and Method

KOSPI daily closes (2024-01-02 to 2026-06-26, 604 days) and investor-type daily net flows were collected from Naver Finance. D_sell is set from the foreign/institutional net-flow signs. The backtest comprises four hypotheses: H1 (contemporaneous classification), H2 (crash capture), H3 (next-day forecast, ex-ante), H4 (trading, prior-day signal, no look-ahead).

6. Limitations and Conclusion

Limitations: (1) no next-day point forecast — intraday data required; (2) single market, 2.5-year sample; (3) K_cascade/C_flow calibrated in-sample; (4) trading costs excluded.

Conclusion: the RC monotonic-recovery assumption is physically refuted. The diode model classifies market direction at t = 9.55 over 604 days and demonstrates that the institutional sign is a superior switch to the foreign gate. The next step (v6) is to capture the D_sell ignition timing from intraday 30-minute cumulative foreign/institutional net flows, and to integrate Codex's multi-US-signal scheme into V_target.
